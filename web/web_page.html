The Everyday Problem

We all save places.
Most of us never go.

Travel planning today is scattered, time-consuming, and often leaves people more confused than when they started. Here's what that actually looks like.

📱

Too much saved, too little structure

Many people save dozens of travel posts before a trip — restaurants, photo spots, local events. But there's no easy way to turn all of that into a workable plan. So the posts just sit there, unused.

"I have so many saved posts and I never actually use them."
🧩

Everything is disconnected

People switch between Google Maps, Yelp, transit apps, and social media just to plan one afternoon. Nothing talks to each other. The result is wasted time and unnecessary stress.

"I'm juggling five apps and I still don't know if I can afford it all."
💸

No idea what it'll actually cost

Budget is often an afterthought in travel planning — you find out how much you've spent after the fact, not before. This is especially difficult for students and budget-conscious travellers.

"I had no idea I'd go over budget until I was already there."
🌍

Newcomers and visitors get left behind

If you don't have local knowledge — or someone to ask — it's hard to know where to start, what's worth visiting, and what to skip. Most travel apps surface the same tourist spots for everyone.

"I didn't know what to do first, or even how to get there."
Why This Matters

The way we discover places
has changed.
Planning hasn't caught up.

Social media changed how we find things to do. But it was never built to help us actually plan. That gap is the problem TripTailor is trying to solve.

📲

Discovery happens on social media

People find restaurants, events, and neighbourhoods on Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube — not travel agencies. But those platforms weren't designed to help you plan around a budget or figure out transit.

🌆

Cities are harder to navigate without local knowledge

Pop-up events, complex transit systems, and constantly changing neighbourhoods make planning a visit genuinely difficult — especially if you're new to a city.

⏰

Time is limited, and planning takes too long

Busy students, working adults, and families with kids don't have hours to spend researching a Saturday out. But if you don't plan properly, you often end up doing nothing at all.

💚

Better planning means more access for everyone

When planning is easier, more people explore — and that's good for communities, local businesses, and the sense of belonging in a city.

Honest Answers · Potential Concerns

Fair questions.
Straight answers.

That's a fair question — and the honest answer is: kind of, but not quite. Most travel apps tell you about places. TripTailor takes your list of places and helps you actually plan around them — with budget, transit, and food all integrated. The problem isn't that there's too few travel apps. It's that none of them do the planning for you.

You absolutely can — and most people do. But Google Maps doesn't know your budget, and social media doesn't give you transit times. TripTailor isn't about replacing those tools; it's about connecting them in a way that saves you an hour of manual work before a day out.

TripTailor is a planning assistant, not an authority. Every route shows you how it was built — commute times come from real transit data, costs are estimates, and you can edit or remove any stop. Think of it as a 80% head start, not the final word. You're always in control.

This is something we think about a lot. Our curation approach actively tries to surface local spots — independent restaurants, neighbourhood markets, community events — alongside major attractions. We're also exploring ways to give local businesses fair visibility rather than just pushing well-known names. It's a design challenge we're actively working on.

Privacy is a core design consideration, not an afterthought. The concept is built around minimal data collection — your saved routes and preferences stay on-device by default. As a university project, we're committed to privacy-by-design principles. Any data features in a future product would be opt-in and clearly explained.

Not yet — TripTailor is currently a university project prototype. What you're seeing is a high-fidelity demonstration of the concept and how it could work. We're being transparent about that. If there's genuine public interest and the project develops beyond the classroom, we'll be open about what comes next. Your feedback on this page is exactly what helps us understand whether that's worthwhile.

Most of our user research has been with locals and students — not tourists. TripTailor is often more useful for people who know a city but haven't explored it fully. If you're trying to make better use of your weekends, discover new neighbourhoods, or host visiting family without spending hours on Google, it's designed exactly for that.
